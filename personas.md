# Toro Movers — Customer Personas

A 5-persona library covering the actual Toro Movers customer base. Built to inform ad targeting, copy decisions, landing page strategy, and content planning.

**How to use this document:**
- Each persona represents a real type of customer with a distinct job-to-be-done.
- Don't try to make one ad / one page serve all 5 — build dedicated assets per persona.
- The cross-reference matrix at the bottom maps each persona to its best landing page, package, and ad angle.

---

## Persona 1 — DIY Danielle

> *"I can drive the truck, but there's no way I can move my couch and all those heavy boxes by myself. I just need a couple of strong, reliable guys to handle the tough stuff so I don't break my back or my belongings."*

### Snapshot
Late-20s marketing professional in downtown Orlando. Rented a U-Haul to save money, needs strong hands for the heavy lifting. The OG labor-only customer.

### Demographics
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Age | 26-32 |
| Location | Orlando (Thornton Park, Mills 50, SoDo, Audubon Park, MetroWest) |
| Occupation | Marketing coordinator, junior creative, early-career professional |
| Income | $50K-$70K |
| Family | Single or partnered, no children |
| Home | Renting a 1BR apartment, moving to another rental or first home purchase |

### Job-to-be-done
Get her belongings loaded into a rented truck quickly, safely, and without injury — at the lowest reasonable price.

### Budget
**$300-$600** (Loading Help package: 2 movers × 2-3 hours, no truck)

### Search behavior
She types these into Google:
1. `labor only movers orlando`
2. `loading help orlando fl`
3. `movers to load u-haul orlando`
4. `moving help orlando $75 hour`
5. `cheap movers orlando reviews`

### Channels (where to reach her)
- **Google Search** — primary, very high intent
- **Google Reviews / Yelp** — research before booking
- **Reddit** (r/orlando, r/CentralFlorida) — vetting + recommendations
- **Nextdoor** — neighborhood recommendations
- **Meta ads** — works for retargeting; lookalike to past Loading Help bookers

### Top 3 objections
1. **"Will they actually show up on time?"** — same-week scheduling and on-time guarantee need to be visible
2. **"Are they real movers or just guys with a truck?"** — insurance, FL license, review count are critical
3. **"What if they damage my stuff?"** — furniture wrapping included + insurance proof on request

### Best landing page
`/orlando-movers` (also `/maitland-movers`, `/winter-park-movers` for nearby variants)

### Best package
**Loading Help — $300** (2 movers × 2 hours)

### Ad creative angle
> **"$300. Two movers. Two hours. You drive the U-Haul. We handle the heavy stuff."**

Visual: Hero shot of crew loading boxes into a U-Haul. Price point is the hook. "$300" should be the largest text in the ad.

### Content priorities
- Make Loading Help package prominent on the page
- Calculator above the fold (she wants to self-serve a quote)
- Reviews near the CTA
- "Same-week scheduling" badge

## Persona 2 — Relocation Rachel

> *"I just want one company to handle the whole thing — show up, load it, drive it, unload it, done. I don't want to deal with U-Haul, and I definitely don't want surprise charges at the end. Tell me the price up front and stick to it."*

### Snapshot
Early-30s professional moving from a 1BR apartment to a 2BR townhome with her partner. Wants the truck included so she doesn't have to coordinate a rental. Has been burned by hidden fees before.

### Demographics
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Age | 28-36 |
| Location | Lake Mary, Sanford, Winter Park, Oviedo, Winter Springs, Altamonte Springs, Casselberry |
| Occupation | Nurse, teacher, paralegal, office professional, account manager |
| Income | $60K-$95K (single or household) |
| Family | Partnered, possibly engaged, 0-1 kids |
| Home | Moving from 1BR apartment → 1-2BR townhome, condo, or first house |

### Job-to-be-done
Move her household across Central Florida in one day with no truck rental hassle, no surprise fees, and no damage to recently-purchased furniture.

### Budget
**$700-$1,200** (In-Town Move package: 2 movers × 4 hours + truck included)

### Search behavior
1. `movers lake mary fl`
2. `small moving company orlando 2 bedroom`
3. `1 bedroom movers central florida`
4. `affordable movers winter park`
5. `honest movers orlando no hidden fees`

### Channels
- **Google Search + Google Maps** — local pack rankings matter heavily
- **Google Reviews** — reads 5-15 reviews before booking
- **Meta/Instagram ads** — visual content works well for this demo
- **Friends and family referrals** — word of mouth is strong
- **Realtor referrals** — if she's buying her first home

### Top 3 objections
1. **"Hidden fees"** — fuel surcharges, fuel fees, distance fees, stair fees, booking fees. Has been burned.
2. **"Last-minute reschedules"** — needs reliability around her work schedule
3. **"Damage to recent purchases"** — most of her furniture is West Elm / Wayfair, not heirlooms, but it's expensive

### Best landing pages
`/lake-mary-movers`, `/sanford-movers`, `/oviedo-movers`, `/winter-park-movers`, `/winter-springs-movers`, `/altamonte-springs-movers`

### Best package
**In-Town Move — $875** (2 movers × 4 hours + truck)

### Ad creative angle
> **"$875. We bring the truck. Two movers, four hours, zero surprises."**

Visual: Crew loading furniture into the Toro Movers truck. Emphasize "We bring the truck" — that's her primary pain reduction.

### Content priorities
- "No hidden fees" promise prominent on page
- In-Town Move package featured (it's the "Most Popular" badge for a reason)
- Calculator with truck add-on visible
- Reviews highlighting "exactly what they quoted" testimonials
- License + insured trust signals

## Persona 3 — Family Frank

> *"We're moving for the schools, so the timing has to be perfect. My wife wants packing help because she's already losing her mind. We've got a piano, the kids' rooms, and a garage full of stuff. We just need someone we can trust to show up, do it right, and not nickel-and-dime us at the end."*

### Snapshot
Late-30s to mid-40s family man relocating his family of 4 from a 3BR home to a 4BR in a better school district. Wife is overwhelmed and wants packing help. They have a piano. They have a dog. They have a garage full of stuff.

### Demographics
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Age | 36-48 |
| Location | Maitland, Winter Park, Lake Mary, Heathrow, Windermere, Ocoee, Apopka, Winter Garden, Oviedo |
| Occupation | IT manager, small business owner, mid-career professional, healthcare admin |
| Income | $90K-$160K (household) |
| Family | Married, 2 kids, dog or two |
| Home | Moving from 3BR → 4BR, school-district-driven |

### Job-to-be-done
Move the entire family with as little disruption as possible. Wife wants packing help. Kids' rooms need to be packed last and unpacked first. Closing day timing is critical.

### Budget
**$1,200-$2,500** (Big Move + packing add-on)

### Search behavior
1. `best movers orlando family`
2. `full service movers winter park`
3. `movers and packers orlando`
4. `professional movers maitland fl`
5. `licensed insured moving company orlando`
6. `4 bedroom movers central florida`

### Channels
- **Google Search** — primary, heavy researcher
- **Google Reviews** — reads 10+ reviews, looks at 1-star reviews specifically
- **Meta ads** — Facebook more than Instagram for this demo
- **School parent Facebook groups** — strong word-of-mouth channel
- **Neighborhood Nextdoor** — checks for recommendations and warnings
- **Realtor referrals** — if buying, his realtor is a key referral source

### Top 3 objections
1. **"Will they handle our valuable stuff carefully?"** — has a piano, antiques inherited from parents, large TV, kids' bunk beds
2. **"Cost transparency"** — has been quoted $4K+ by national chains and is suspicious of all moving companies
3. **"Timing reliability"** — closing day timing is non-negotiable; can't be late

### Best landing pages
`/maitland-movers`, `/winter-park-movers`, `/lake-mary-movers`, `/windermere-movers`, `/winter-garden-movers`, `/oviedo-movers`, `/apopka-movers`, `/ocoee-movers`

### Best package
**Big Move — $1,175** + packing custom quote

### Ad creative angle
> **"Family-owned movers for family-sized moves. Packing, loading, driving, unloading — one fair hourly rate, no surprises."**

Visual: Family/team dock portrait (the current hero photo!) — emphasizes family business, builds trust with another family.

### Content priorities
- Big Move package + the new packing/long-distance/storage callout strip
- Packing & unpacking FAQ entries (already there)
- Trust signals stacked: licensed, insured, family-owned, 4.9★
- Real testimonials from 3-bedroom moves
- Calculator showing 3+ movers + 4+ hours configurations

## Persona 4 — Downsizing Dolores

> *"I've lived in this house for 38 years. My grandmother's china cabinet is in the dining room. I need movers who understand that I'm not just moving boxes — I'm closing a chapter. I need someone patient, careful, and honest about what it'll cost."*

### Snapshot
65-75 year-old retiree leaving the family home of 30+ years. Moving into a smaller place — patio home in Heathrow, The Villages, an active-adult community in Clermont, or back closer to her adult children up north. Every piece of furniture has meaning.

### Demographics
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Age | 63-78 |
| Location | Winter Park, Maitland, Heathrow (Lake Mary), The Villages, Clermont (Kings Ridge / Heritage Hills / Summit Greens), Sanford, Casselberry |
| Occupation | Retired (former teacher, nurse, executive, business owner) |
| Income | Fixed income or modest savings + Social Security |
| Family | Widowed, divorced, or married — kids are adults living elsewhere |
| Home | Moving from 3-4BR family home → smaller patio home, condo, assisted living, OR moving back near adult children up north |

### Job-to-be-done
Move out of a long-held home with respect, patience, and care for furniture that has decades of emotional value. Often needs help sorting (keep / family / donate / storage). May need long-distance.

### Budget
**$1,500-$3,000** (Big Move + extra hours for slow handling, possibly long-distance with truck fee)

### Search behavior

**Her own searches:**
1. `senior moving services orlando`
2. `downsizing movers central florida`
3. `movers winter park antiques`
4. `movers the villages florida`
5. `senior friendly moving company orlando`

**Her adult children's searches** (often the actual decision-maker):
1. `movers for elderly parent orlando`
2. `downsizing services for parents florida`
3. `long distance movers florida to [northern state]`
4. `movers the villages reviews`
5. `senior moving services reviews orlando`

### Channels
- **Google Search** — uses, but slowly
- **Google Reviews** — trusts review counts heavily; will not book a 4-star or below
- **Adult children doing research on her behalf** — *this is the real decision pathway*
- **Senior community newsletters and bulletin boards**
- **Doctor's office / physical therapist referrals** — high-trust source
- **Real estate agents** — when listing the home
- **Estate sale companies** — referral partners
- **Print advertising** — still works for this demo

### Top 3 objections
1. **"Will they treat my things with care?"** — every piece has meaning
2. **"Will they rush me?"** — she needs to go slow, ask questions, change her mind, take breaks
3. **"Will they be honest with me about the cost?"** — fixed income, can't afford surprises, has been targeted by scams before

### Best landing pages
`/the-villages-movers` (perfect for downsizing into a Village)
`/winter-park-movers` (the "We handle history with care" angle is built for her)
`/clermont-movers` (Kings Ridge / Heritage Hills / Summit Greens active-adult communities)
`/lake-mary-movers` (Heathrow patio homes)
`/sanford-movers` (Historic Downtown with antiques)

### Best package
**Big Move — $1,175** OR **Custom Quote** for long-distance

### Ad creative angle
> **"Patient, family-owned movers who treat your home of 40 years with respect. Same crew. Fair rate. No rush."**

Visual: Crew carefully wrapping a piece of furniture in shrink wrap. Older customer in frame nodding approvingly.

### Content priorities
- The "We handle history with care" angle from `/winter-park-movers` is the gold standard
- Downsizing FAQ (the one in `/the-villages-movers`)
- Trust signals: family-owned, 4.9★ from 29 reviews, licensed, insured
- Voice-of-customer testimonials from older customers specifically
- Phone number large and prominent (she'll call before she'll fill out a form)

### ⚠️ Critical targeting note
**Adult children are often the actual decision-maker.** Meta ad targeting should include lookalikes of "adult child of senior" demographics (age 35-55, parents in Florida, search history around senior services). Run a separate ad set targeting adult children with messaging like "Help your parents move out of their long-held home with patience and respect."

## Persona 5 — Vacation-Rental Vanessa

> *"I'm in Boston. My ChampionsGate property has new owners moving the old furniture out and new furniture coming in next Thursday. I need a moving company I can trust to handle the whole thing, coordinate with the cleaning crew, and send me photos so I know it went smoothly. Same hourly rate is great — I just need it to actually happen."*

### Snapshot
Out-of-state vacation rental investor with 1-3 properties in the Disney corridor. Needs furniture turnovers, deliveries, and disposal coordinated remotely. Won't be on-site. Trust and communication are everything.

### Demographics
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Age | 32-50 |
| Location | Lives outside Florida (Northeast US, Toronto, UK, sometimes Tampa Bay) |
| Occupation | Real estate investor, tech professional, small business owner with side investment portfolio |
| Income | $120K-$400K |
| Family | Married/partnered, kids vary |
| Home | Owns 1-3 vacation rental properties in Davenport, ChampionsGate, Kissimmee (Solterra/Storey Lake), Windsor Hills, Reunion |

### Job-to-be-done
Coordinate furniture turnovers, deliveries, and disposal between guest stays without being on-site. Needs reliability + remote communication + photo proof.

### Budget
**$1,000-$3,000 per turnover, recurring** (often 1-2x per year per property)

### Search behavior
1. `vacation rental movers davenport`
2. `airbnb furniture movers orlando`
3. `champions gate moving company`
4. `short term rental movers central florida`
5. `vacation home turnover service florida`
6. `solterra resort movers`

### Channels
- **Google Search** — research-heavy, often before ever visiting Florida
- **BiggerPockets, Airhosts forums** — where she vets vendors
- **Vacation rental owner Facebook groups** — peer recommendations
- **Property management company referrals** — high-trust channel
- **Email marketing** — works well for this segment
- **Google Maps / Reviews** — once she has a shortlist

### Top 3 objections
1. **"Trust without being on-site"** — can't supervise, needs reliable communication and proof
2. **"Coordination with other vendors"** — cleaners, pool company, HVAC, often all on the same day
3. **"Insurance and damage liability"** — high-end furniture, short-term rental margins are thin, can't absorb damage

### Best landing pages
`/davenport-movers` (already explicitly addresses ChampionsGate, Solterra, Reunion, Windsor Hills)
`/kissimmee-movers` (Solterra, Storey Lake, gated community angle)

### Best package
**Custom Quote** (typically Big Move + extra hours for turnover work)

### Ad creative angle
> **"Reliable vacation home turnovers in ChampionsGate, Solterra, and Reunion. We coordinate with your cleaning crew. Photos sent to you. Done by check-in."**

Visual: Crew arranging furniture in a vacation home setting. Show photo-proof / communication aspect.

### Content priorities
- Davenport page already addresses this customer well (already there)
- Could add a dedicated "vacation rental owners" landing section or callout
- "We send photos" promise (a new feature you'd commit to delivering)
- Email marketing list capture for vacation rental owners
- Outreach to property management companies for referral partnerships

### Recurring revenue note
This is the only persona with **recurring revenue potential** — if you turn over a property once, you'll likely turn it over again. Build a "preferred vendor" relationship offer (e.g., 5% off for repeat customers, priority scheduling, dedicated point of contact).

# Recommended Next Steps

Now that you have personas, here's what to do with them:

1. **Set up 5 Meta ad campaigns** — one per persona, each with persona-specific creative, targeting, and landing page.
2. **Set up 5 Google Ads campaigns** — same structure, with persona-specific keywords.
3. **Add a "vacation rental owners" callout block** to the Davenport and Kissimmee pages for Vanessa.
4. **Add an "adult children of seniors" landing page or section** for Dolores's adult child decision pathway.
5. **Create persona-specific email sequences** for any leads that don't book immediately — especially Vanessa (recurring revenue) and Dolores (long sales cycle).
6. **Track conversions by persona via the form `source-page` field** that's already in place — over time you'll see which personas actually convert from which pages.
