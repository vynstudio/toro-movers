Moving in Avalon Park? From historic homes to Auto Train day.

Avalon Park is one of Central Florida's oldest cities, and it shows in the moves. The brick streets of Historic Downtown lead to bungalows that have stood since the 1920s — careful doorways, narrow stairs, antiques worth handling slowly. Out by Lake Monroe, lakefront homes have heavy custom furniture that takes a real crew to move. And every week, families show up at the Auto Train terminal needing their car loaded and the rest of their household packed and shipped.

We're a family-owned moving company that knows Avalon Park — Historic Downtown, Lake Forest, the corridors out toward Heathrow, the developments along Rinehart Road. Whether you're moving from a downtown bungalow into a Lake Mary new-build, or relocating up north on the Auto Train, the rate is the same and the crew shows up on time.

FAQ

Common questions, honest answers.

Do you serve all of Avalon Park?

Yes — every neighborhood. We move families in Historic Downtown, Lake Forest, Lake Monroe area, Rinehart Road, Heathrow border, Mayfair, Loch Arbor, Celery Estates, Goldsboro, Wynwood, and everywhere in between. Same hourly rate, no zone fees, no fuel surcharges.

How much does a Avalon Park move cost?

$75 per mover, per hour, with a 2-hour minimum. A typical 1-bedroom Avalon Park move runs about $300-$500 (2 movers, 2-3 hours). A 2-bedroom move with our truck runs about $700-$900. A larger historic home with antiques runs about $1,000-$1,400 (extra wrapping time, more careful handling). Use the calculator above or call us for a custom quote.

Do you do Auto Train relocations?

Yes — Avalon Park's Auto Train terminal is one of the most common pickups we handle. We pack up your house, load your car onto the Auto Train, and ship the rest of your belongings to your destination separately. We coordinate the timing so everything arrives within a day or two of each other. Same $75/mover/hour rate.

Can you move antiques in historic downtown Avalon Park?

Yes — Historic Downtown is one of our favorite areas to work. We've moved generations of family pieces out of 1920s bungalows without a scratch. Our crew wraps every antique with blankets and shrink wrap, measures doorways before lifting, and disassembles anything that won't fit through century-old openings.

What if the move takes longer than I booked?

Easy — we just keep going at $75 per mover per hour. No re-booking, no surprise fees. You only pay for the time we actually work.

What if my move is shorter than 2 hours?

The 2-hour minimum still applies. Most moves take longer than people think, and we want our crew compensated for showing up. If you finish faster, you've still got us on-site for the full window.

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes — fully licensed and insured in Florida. We can send you proof of insurance and our license number on request. Just ask.

What if you damage something?

We're insured for it, and our crews are trained to wrap furniture before moving it. In hundreds of moves, we've kept claims to a minimum and resolved them all directly with the customer.

Do you bring tools and supplies?

Yes — dollies, blankets, straps, and basic tools to disassemble and reassemble standard furniture. All free, included in the hourly rate.

Do you do packing and unpacking?

Yes — we can pack a whole house, just the kitchen, or just the fragile stuff. Same $75 per mover, per hour. We bring boxes, packing paper, and tape on request (small materials fee for supplies). Tell us what you need when you book.

Do you do long-distance moves?

Yes — anywhere in Florida and out of state. Same hourly rate for the crew, plus a flat truck fee based on the distance. We give you a transparent custom quote up front so there are no surprises on moving day.

Can you move my storage unit?

Absolutely. Moving in or out of a storage unit is one of the most common jobs we do. Climate-controlled, ground-level, or stairs-up — same hourly rate. Just tell us the unit address and access details when you book.

How do I pay?

A small deposit holds your slot when you book. The balance is paid after the job by card, Cash App, or Zelle. No payment up front for the full amount.
